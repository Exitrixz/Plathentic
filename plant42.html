Aquilegia Vulgaris

Common Columbine

Watering (L): Moderate, keep soil evenly moist

Properties: Herbaceous perennial, delicate foliage, nodding flowers

Edible: No

Poisonous: Yes, all parts are toxic if ingested

Colors: Various colors including blue, pink, purple, white, yellow

Preferred Season: Spring to early summer

Found in Biome: Woodlands, gardens

Possible Diseases: Powdery mildew, leaf spot

Disease Treatment: Fungicides, remove affected foliage

Pruning: Deadhead spent flowers to encourage reblooming

Fertilizer: Balanced fertilizer during active growth

CAUTION: All parts of Aquilegia vulgaris are toxic if ingested. Handle with care and keep away from children and pets.
