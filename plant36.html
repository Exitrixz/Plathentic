Amorphophallus Konjac

Konjac

Watering (L): Moderate, keep soil evenly moist but not waterlogged

Properties: Perennial herb, tuberous root system

Edible: Yes, tubers used in Asian cuisine (konjac noodles)

Poisonous: No, but raw tubers contain toxic compounds if not processed

Colors: Green leaves, maroon to purple flowers

Preferred Season: Summer

Found in Biome: Forest floors, cultivated in gardens

Possible Diseases: Root rot if overwatered, fungal infections

Disease Treatment: Improve drainage, fungicides

Pruning: Remove dead leaves and flowers to encourage growth

Fertilizer: Balanced fertilizer during growing season, reduce in winter
