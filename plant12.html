Aconitum Carmichaelii

Carmichael's Monkshood

Watering (L): 1-2 liters per day

Properties: Perennial herb, deeply lobed leaves, tall spikes of hooded flowers

Edible: No

Poisonous: Yes, highly toxic if ingested or handled improperly

Colors: Blue, purple, or white flowers, green leaves

Preferred Season: Late summer to early autumn

Found in Biome: Mountain meadows, woodlands

Possible Diseases: Root rot, powdery mildew

Disease Treatment: Fungicides, proper air circulation and drainage

Pruning: Cut back after flowering to encourage new growth

Fertilizer: Balanced, slow-release fertilizer in spring
